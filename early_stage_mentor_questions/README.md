# Early Stage Fintech Mentor Questions

A structured set of high-value questions for mentoring early-stage fintech hackathon teams. These are designed to force clarity, validate feasibility, and expose weak assumptions.

---

## 1. Problem Definition & Market Fit

- Who exactly is your target user segment?
- What specific problem are you solving?
- Can you describe a real-world scenario where this problem occurs?
- How is this problem currently solved?
- Why is this problem worth solving now?
- What is the cost of inaction for the user?

---

## 2. Value Proposition & Differentiation

- Why would users switch to your solution?
- What is your core value proposition in one sentence?
- Who are your top 3 competitors?
- What do competitors do better than you?
- What is your unfair advantage?
- Is your solution significantly better or just slightly improved?

---

## 3. Business Model & Economics

- How do you make money?
- Who is the paying customer?
- What is your expected pricing model?
- What are your main cost drivers?
- What is your rough estimate of customer acquisition cost (CAC)?
- What is the expected lifetime value (LTV) of a customer?

---

## 4. Data Strategy

- What data do you need for your solution to work?
- Do you currently have access to this data?
- If not, how will you obtain it?
- What is the quality and reliability of the data?
- How frequently is the data updated?
- What happens if the data is incomplete or unavailable?

---

## 5. Technical Feasibility

- What is your high-level system architecture?
- What are the main components of your system?
- What is your MVP versus full vision?
- What are the top 3 technical challenges?
- Can your MVP realistically be built within the hackathon timeframe?

---

## 6. Security & Compliance

- What sensitive data are you handling?
- How will you store and protect that data?
- Are you subject to GDPR or other regulations?
- Do you require KYC or AML processes?
- Are you acting as a financial intermediary?

---

## 7. Integration & Ecosystem

- What external systems do you depend on?
- Do these systems provide APIs?
- What are the limitations of these APIs?
- What happens if an external service fails?
- Are there alternative providers?

---

## 8. UX & Trust

- Why would users trust your platform?
- What is the onboarding process?
- What is the first interaction a user has with your product?
- How do you communicate value quickly to users?
- Where might users drop off or lose trust?

---

## 9. Scalability & Growth

- What happens if you scale to 10,000 users?
- What components will break first?
- What are your main infrastructure costs?
- How will your system handle increased load?
- Is your architecture stateless or stateful, and why?

---

## 10. Execution Plan

- What will you deliver in this hackathon?
- What is your week-by-week plan?
- What is your demo scenario?
- What features are you intentionally not building?
- What is your smallest viable product?

---

## 11. Evaluation & Metrics

- How will you measure success?
- What is your primary (north star) metric?
- What indicates product failure?
- How will you validate user adoption?
- What early signals will you track?

---

## 12. Kill Shot Questions (Use Sparingly)

- If a major bank builds this tomorrow, what happens to your idea?
- What single assumption, if false, completely breaks your solution?
- What is the hardest technical part of your system?
- Are you building a feature or a company?
- Why has this not already been solved?
